Scorecard and Pilot Feedback

Groundbreaking New Wildfire Resilience Scorecard Empowers Communities to Assess Wildfire Risk, Boost Preparedness
Global Rollout Kicks Off With Pilot Program to Test Methodology. Participants Wanted
X, X, 2023, (San Francisco, CA) --- Communities worldwide now have an invaluable new tool to thoroughly evaluate wildfire vulnerability and strategically strengthen resilience.

The groundbreaking ARISE-US/CrowdDoing Catastrophic Wildfire Prevention & Consortium (CWP&C) Community Wildfire Resilience Scorecard enables a comprehensive assessment of wildfire risk across property, community and landscape scales. Developed collaboratively and peer-reviewed by wildfire experts in ARISE-US and CrowdDoing CWP&C, the scorecard provides a robust set of questions to develop a baseline assessment, identify risks, and contribute to creating targeted preparedness plans, and driving unified action.

Download the Community Wildfire Resilience Scorecard, published on the United Nations’ Disaster Risk & Resilience (UNDRR), Prevention Web site.

The ARISE-US/CrowdDoing CWP&C Community Wildfire Resilience Scorecard framework incorporates cutting-edge resilience principles and a diverse range of readiness factors. These include land use planning, infrastructure hardening, public education, governance, nature-based solutions, ecosystem management, and post-disaster recovery capacity.

The scorecard prepares a community to develop a prioritized action plan to substantially reduce wildfire risks through integrated planning, mitigation and governance. This tool has immense potential to galvanize stakeholders across public, private and civic spheres. Together, we can build collaborative resilience and catastrophic wildfire risk reduction strategies worldwide against increasingly devastating wildfires.

The global rollout kicks off with a pilot program enabling municipalities worldwide to test and refine the Wildfire Resilience Scorecard.

"By building systems change strategies in collaboration with diverse global virtual collaborators we can become more effective at catastrophic wildfire risk reduction. Through this approach, we can empower communities to take strategic action to reduce wildfire risks and build pathways of prevention based on understanding their risks and capabilities through use of this scorecard. Together, we can create more resilient communities ready to thrive in our hotter, drier, more fire-prone world," said Bobby Fishkin, Co- CEO CrowdDoing CWP&C.
This is only the beginning for The ARISE-US/CrowdDoing CWP&C Community Wildfire Resilience Scorecard collaborative development.
The ARISE-US/CrowdDoing Catastrophic Wildfire Prevention & Consortium team invites a wide variety of stakeholders, including the public and private sector, to adopt this scorecard, pilot their own deeper understanding of their risk and resilience, and to consider helping us to improve the scorecard with feedback, pilots and collaboration.

In addition to local communities, example stakeholders include:

  • Local, regional, and national governments
  • Insurance companies
  • Reinsurance companies/ Insurance linked security companies
  • Utility companies
  • Social enterprises
  • Neighborhood and home-owner associations
  • Developers/real estate/ landowners
  • Community wildfire organizations
  • Local community emergency preparedness associations etc.

All communities are expected to have gaps in catastrophic wildfire community risk reduction. The objective is to anticipate how vulnerabilities to wildfire generate opportunities for catastrophe community risk reduction and a chance to learn from others who have already embraced some of these strategies. We aspire to amplify network effects in the form of accelerated learning from many regions creatively reducing their risks and sharing their results in a community of practice.
The ARISE-US/CrowdDoing CWP&C Community Wildfire Resilience Scorecard has been released as an open source tool.
